Behavioral biometrics identifies users based on how they interact with devices rather than what they know or have. Every person has a unique way of moving their mouse, scrolling, clicking, and typing.

When an attacker compromises credentials, they cannot mimic the legitimate user's behavioral patterns. The mouse will move differently, clicks will happen at unusual intervals, and keystroke dynamics will not match.

Behavioral monitoring detects anomalies indicating account takeover, bot activity, or insider threats by comparing a session's interaction patterns against a learned baseline.

Key metrics include mouse movement velocity and acceleration, scroll pacing and delta, click frequency and spatial distribution, and keystroke timing dynamics including hold duration and inter-key intervals.

Combined, these signals create a robust behavioral profile that adapts over time as the user's natural behavior evolves, while still flagging sudden departures that suggest a different human or bot is at the keyboard.
